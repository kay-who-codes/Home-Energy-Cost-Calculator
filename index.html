How It Works

Every electrical appliance has a power rating in watts (W). Gas appliances use a different unit — kilowatts (kW). To find the cost of running anything for one hour, you multiply its power by your energy tariff (how much you pay per unit of energy).

⚡ Electricity

Measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh). A 100W bulb uses 0.1 kWh per hour. You pay per kWh — typically around 24–29p in the UK.

🔥 Gas

Gas is also billed in kWh. A typical gas boiler outputs 24–30 kW. Hot water and radiators each use a fraction of that. UK gas is ~6–7p/kWh.

📐 The Formula

Watts ÷ 1000 = kilowatts. Kilowatts × hours = kWh used. kWh × tariff = cost in pence (divide by 100 for £).

Cost (£) = ( Watts ÷ 1000 ) × Hours × Tariff (p/kWh) ÷ 100

💡 Money-Saving Tips

🔌Switch to LED bulbs — a 9W LED replaces a 60W incandescent, saving over 80% on lighting costs.
🌡️Turning your thermostat down by 1°C can cut your heating bill by around 10% per year.
🚰A power shower uses more energy than your boiler heating a bath. Short showers cost less than you think.
🧊A full fridge is more efficient than a half-empty one — the food acts as thermal mass.
📋Check your energy bill for your actual p/kWh rate — tariffs vary widely by supplier and plan.
⏱️Use a smart meter or plug-in energy monitor to measure your appliances' real wattage accurately.
Calculations are estimates based on rated wattage × tariff. Actual consumption may vary. UK price cap rates used as defaults. Always check your own bill.
